Play Perl

Play Perl is a new website for Perl developers.

Concepts

Opensource, gamified

Gamification is great for tuning people motivations in a positive way.
Play Perl gives you points and achievements to be proud of.

Social Todo List

Play Perl allows you to announce your plans, gather the feedback, and find other people to cooperate with on common goals.

FAQ

"Quest"? "Player"? What is this?
Quest is any kind of task you're planning to do. Player is you, and other people are players too. You get 1+{number-of-likes} points for each quest you close.
There are many other bug trackers! How is Play Perl different?
Play Perl considers people to be more important than projects, and fun to be more important than planning. We're going to optimize its mechanics for:
  1. People feeling appreciated;
  2. Praise being granted and received wherever and whenever necessary;
  3. Contribution simplified as much as possible.
There are many more ideas for future features, check out our DesignDraft.
Is this for Perl 5 or for Perl 6?
Both.
And also Moe, p2 and anything else that will grow out of Perl community in the future.
Can I use this for my non-Perl tasks?
Please don't.
Here's the short list of rules for possible quests:
  • all quests are good if they're related to Perl somehow;
  • self-referential quests such as "get one more point" or "get to the top of players list" are not ok for obvious reasons;
  • quests not relevant to Perl are temporarily discouraged, because I didn't figure out yet how to a) allow them; b) keep the game points meaningful.

On the other hand, don't hesitate to create a quest even if you feel like the task is too small and not worth a point. Anything that helps you to move forward is fine, and points=likes+1 rule ensures that everyone, in theory, will get a fair score in the end.

This doesn't motivate me.
That's ok. People are different.
I can't use this without feature X.
Why don't you implement it yourself?
How can I add a link to a CPAN module in comments easily?
If your module name contains ::, it'll turn into the link to MetaCPAN automatically. Use cpan:Foo syntax for one-word module names.

Technology

Play Perl is built with:
  • Dancer
  • Backbone.js
  • Bootstrap
  • MongoDB

Backend is completely separated from the frontend. Backend API is documented here, feel free to use it.
(But please don't abuse game mechanics or you'll be banned.)

Play Perl sources are open. Clone it on GitHub!
